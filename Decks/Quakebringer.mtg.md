Quakebringer is a mono-red damage deck built around Giants and repeatable pressure. The deck uses cheap burn to interact early, then turns the corner with Calamity Bearer, Quakebringer, Basalt Ravager, and Squash.

The list is not just a burn pile: it has several threats that keep damage flowing through removal. Quakebringer can drain from the graveyard while a Giant is controlled, Rekindling Phoenix is difficult to remove cleanly, Chandra creates unavoidable emblems, and Dragonmaster Outcast can take over long games once the land count is high enough.

* **Calamity Bearer**: Doubles damage from Giant sources, turning Quakebringer, Basalt Ravager, Squash, and combat into sudden lethal pressure.

## Game Plan

The early turns are about staying alive and preserving tempo with Shock, Lightning Bolt, Dual Shot, and Forgotten Cave cycling when extra lands are not needed. Dragonmaster Outcast can come down early as a must-answer threat, but the deck is usually happiest when it can trade resources until its four- and five-mana engines arrive.

From the midgame onward, the goal is to assemble Giant pressure. Calamity Bearer doubles Giant-source damage, Basalt Ravager scales with shared creature types, Squash becomes a cheap six-damage answer when a Giant is in play, and Quakebringer turns every upkeep into damage the opponent cannot erase with life gain.

<details>
<summary>Detailed Strategy</summary>
<br>

Calamity Bearer is the deck's highest-leverage card. With it on the battlefield, Quakebringer attacks for 10, Basalt Ravager can deal twice its entered value, and Squash becomes 12 damage to a creature or planeswalker if the source is a Giant you control. Sequence Calamity Bearer carefully when the opponent is likely to have removal, because untapping with it can change the game's clock immediately.

Quakebringer gives the deck reach even after combat stalls. Its graveyard trigger matters because Basalt Ravager and Calamity Bearer are Giants, so Quakebringer can keep pressuring opponents after removal if another Giant remains on the battlefield. Its life-gain prevention also protects burn math against stabilizing effects.

The non-Giant threats diversify the plan. Rekindling Phoenix resists one-for-one removal and pressures planeswalkers, Dragonmaster Outcast rewards the deck's high land count with Dragon tokens, and Chandra, Awakened Inferno gives inevitability against control while sweeping smaller creatures when needed.

Against creature decks, prioritize cheap burn and Squash to keep the battlefield manageable until the Giant package takes over. Against slower decks, preserve burn for reach when possible, cycle Forgotten Cave to avoid flooding, and lean on Chandra or Quakebringer triggers to force action.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Additional mana details</summary>
<br>

The mana is straightforward mono-red, with 24 Mountains and 4 Forgotten Caves. Forgotten Cave gives the deck some flood protection, which matters because the list plays 28 lands to support five- and six-mana threats and Dragonmaster Outcast's six-land condition.

The curve has a sharp split between one-mana interaction and four-plus-mana payoffs. That makes early burn important: if the deck falls behind before the Giant package arrives, it can struggle to use its expensive threats profitably.

</details>

## Thoughts

The deck's top-end plan is strong, but the curve skips the two- and three-mana slots entirely. Adding a few early Giants, treasure makers, or flexible red interaction could make Calamity Bearer and Squash more consistent without weakening the Quakebringer plan.

The land count supports Dragonmaster Outcast and Chandra, but 28 lands is high for a deck with eight one-mana burn spells plus Dual Shot. If flooding becomes common, trimming a land or two for more cycling, rummage, or mid-curve threats would make the deck smoother.
