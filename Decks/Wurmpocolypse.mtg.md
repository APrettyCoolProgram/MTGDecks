## Overview

<div align="center">

|Deck||
|:--|:--|
|Archetype|Golgari Wurm Ramp|
|Colors|Black/Green|
|Strategy|Ramp and fix mana, survive the midgame, then chain large Wurms and Wurm-token engines|
|Win Condition(s)|Massive Wurm attacks, Roar of the Wurm tokens, Sandwurm Convergence inevitability, and Massacre Wurm life loss|
|Legalities|Brawl/Commander/Duel/Gladiator/Historic/Legacy/Oathbreaker/Timeless/Vintage|

</div>

Wurmpocolypse is a black-green ramp deck built to make the game about oversized creatures. Cultivate and Gift of Paradise push the deck toward six, seven, and eight mana, where Ravenous Lindwurm, Pelakka Wurm, Sifter Wurm, Bookwurm, and Sandwurm Convergence can take over combat.

The black splash gives the deck Massacre Wurm and Dreadwurm. Massacre Wurm punishes opposing creature boards and can turn a cluttered battlefield into a sudden life-total collapse, while Dreadwurm adds landfall pressure that plays naturally with Cultivate.

## Game Plan

The deck wants to spend the early turns developing mana instead of trading one-for-one. Cultivate finds lands, Gift of Paradise fixes and pads life total, and the dual lands keep both colors available for Massacre Wurm and the green top end.

Once the ramp is online, the deck overloads removal and combat with redundant large creatures. Roar of the Wurm and Sandwurm Convergence provide repeatable token pressure, while Massacre Wurm gives the deck a way to punish opponents who try to race with many smaller creatures.

<details>
<summary>Detailed Strategy</summary>
<br>

The strongest hands have early green mana and at least one ramp spell. Cultivate is especially important because it both accelerates and increases land count for Dreadwurm's landfall turns. Gift of Paradise is slower as a ramp spell, but the life gain matters against aggressive decks that try to punish the deck before its six-mana plays come online.

Most of the creature suite is designed to stabilize through size. Ravenous Lindwurm gains life, Siege Wurm can be convoked out with available bodies, Pelakka Wurm stabilizes combat and replaces itself when it dies, and Bookwurm gives the deck a recursive late-game threat. Sifter Wurm adds selection and life gain, which helps smooth high-curve draws.

Massacre Wurm is the main black payoff. It is strongest after the opponent has committed multiple small creatures, and it can end games quickly when combined with combat or token pressure. Because the deck has many expensive spells, avoid spending ramp turns on low-impact plays unless doing so prevents lethal pressure.

Against aggressive decks, prioritize Gift of Paradise, Ravenous Lindwurm, and Massacre Wurm lines that stabilize life total and punish wide boards. Against slower decks, lean on Roar of the Wurm flashback, Bookwurm recursion, and Sandwurm Convergence to keep producing threats after removal.

</details>

<summary>Additional mana details</summary>

The deck is mostly green, and the mana base reflects that with 11 Forests, green dual lands, Cultivate, and Gift of Paradise. Green must be available early because every ramp spell is green.

Black is a splash for Massacre Wurm and Dreadwurm, but both black spells are important. The 5 Swamps, 4 Darkbore Pathways, 2 Overgrown Tombs, 2 Woodland Cemeteries, and Gift of Paradise give the deck enough black access while keeping the green ramp plan intact.

## Thoughts

The deck has a clear identity, but the curve is extremely top-heavy: there are no one- or two-mana nonland plays, and most threats start at five mana. If the list needs to survive faster metagames, the cleanest improvement would be adding early interaction or cheaper ramp while trimming a few redundant six- and seven-mana Wurms.

The mana is functional for Golgari, but the deck is still highly dependent on early green. Additional untapped green sources or lower-cost accelerants would make the big Wurm turns happen more reliably.
